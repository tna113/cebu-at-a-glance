landmarks

Osmeña Peak is Cebu's highest point and is located in Dalaguete. It stands at 1,013 meters above sea level which equtes to 3,323.5 feet in elevation. The hike up to the peak only takes about 20-25 minutes. Kawasan Falls is located in Badian, Cebu and is a three-hour bus ride from Cebu City. Visitors are able to swim in the natural pool, under the waterfall. The site features a restaurant in which visitors can order food and drinks. Mantayupan Falls, alike Kawasan Falls, is another natural springwater waterfall.

animals

Some animals only found in Cebu are: The Black Shama, Uling Gobi, Cebu Hawk Owl, Cebu Small Worm Skink, Kawasan Paper Kite Butterfly and the Cebu Flowerpecker. The Black Shama is a medium-sized bird sighted in most of Central Cebu, specifically within the shrublands of Consolacion. Uling Gobis can be spotted in water pools above waterfalls. The Cebu Hawk Owl is a Cebu-native and can only be found within the island. Cebu's Small Worm Kkink is an elusive, legless skink whose population is currently unknown. You can find the Kawasan Paper Kite Butterfly by visiting the Jumalon Butterfly Sanctuary. The Cebu Fowerpecker is a bird of four colors: white, blueish-black, red and yellowish green.

flowers

Due to Cebu being a tropical island, there are many different types of flowers that are found here. The national flower of the Philippines is the Sampaguita and can be found ubiquitously around Cebu. The Ylang-Ylang flower is commonly found and used in essential oils and perfumes. If you'd like to see the Hanging Lobster Claw Flower, make sure to visit Mountain View! My second favorite flower to see while in Cebu are Bougainvilleas. Their paperlike petals and bright colors are appealing to any passerbyer! They come in many different colors like red, yellow, white and of course, pink. My all-time favorite are Santans, which bloom in red, pink, and yellow.

culture

The main and primary language of the Philippines is Tagalog. All students within the country have this embedded within their school's lesson plans. The second-most spoken/popular language is Cebuano or Visayan. This originated in Cebu and is estimated to be spoken by around 15.8 million people as their first language. The majority of Cebu's residents are Roman Catholic and as a result, Cebuanos tend to celebrate Catholic holidays, traditions and festivities like Christmas, All Saint's Day, Holy Week, All Soul's Day, Easter, and more.

food

By far the best things to do while vacationing abroad is to eat! Jollibee is a famous fast food restaurant chain featuring fried chicken, burgers, Filipino spaghetti (distinctly sweet) and other goodies. This restaurant is extremely popoular (kind of like McDonalds to Americans) so be sure to stop by a branch! Some other famous and foods are: puso (rice in banan leaf), lechon (roasted pig), pochero (beef stew), ngohiong, dangit (dried fish) and chicharon (pork grinds). As for deserts and snacks: puto maya and sikwate, bibinka, masareal, ampaw, budbud, pintos, torta, otap, rosquillos and dried mangoes.

economy

Cebu's economy depends on mostly tourism and the information technology (IT) sector. Specifically, 56% of the island's wealth comes from industry, 36% from services and 8% from agiculture. Since tourism is stimulating a bulk of Cebu's economy, the demand for hotels and resorts are increasing. There are quite a handful of BPOs (Business Process Outsourcing) and most of them are headquartered in Cebu City's IT Park. BPOs call for non-call center work like accounting, legal work, language translation, medical transcription and technical support. Companies like Chase Bank and Facebook are some examples of these businesses.
